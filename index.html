Ingredients:

  • For the pan-seared salmon:
    • 2 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
    • Salt and pepper
    • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • For the avocado salsa:
    • 1 ripe avocado, diced
    • 1/4 red onion, finely chopped
    • 1/2 jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped
    • 1/2 lime, juiced
    • 2 tablespoons cilantro, chopped
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • For the mixed green salad:
    • 3 cups mixed greens
    • 1/4 red onion, thinly sliced
    • 1/4 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
    • 2 tablespoons crumbled feta cheese
    • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinaigrette
  • For the garlic bread:
    • 2 slices of French bread
    • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
    • 1 clove garlic, minced
    • Salt and pepper, to taste
    • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • For the honeydew cucumber cooler:
    • 2 cups honeydew melon, peeled, seeded, and cubed
    • 1/2 cucumber, peeled and chopped
    • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • For the simple and affordable dessert:
    • 4-5 ripe peaches, pitted and sliced
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
    • 1/2 tablespoon unsalted butter
    • Ice cream (optional)

Instructions:

Preheat oven to 400°F.

To make the avocado salsa, combine the diced avocado, chopped red onion, jalapeño, lime juice, and cilantro in a bowl. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

To make the mixed green salad, combine the mixed greens, red onion, cherry tomatoes, feta cheese, and balsamic vinaigrette in a small bowl. Toss to combine.

To make the garlic bread, melt the butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Add the minced garlic and cook for 1-2 minutes, until fragrant. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Brush the mixture over the slices of French bread. Place the slices on a baking sheet and bake for 8-10 minutes, until golden brown. Sprinkle with chopped parsley.

To make the honeydew cucumber cooler, combine the honeydew melon, cucumber, lime juice, sugar, and salt in a blender and blend until smooth. Chill in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

To make the simple and affordable dessert, heat a small skillet over medium heat. Add the sliced peaches, sugar, and cinnamon. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 5-7 minutes, until the peaches are soft and the sugar has caramelized. Stir in the butter until melted. Serve warm with ice cream, if desired.

To make the pan-seared salmon, heat the olive oil in a small skillet over medium-high heat. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper on both sides. Add the salmon fillets to the skillet, skin-side down, and cook for 3-4 minutes, until the skin is crispy. Flip the salmon fillets and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes, until the salmon is cooked through. Serve with the avocado salsa.

Enjoy your meal!
